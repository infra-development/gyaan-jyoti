# Finance Mastery Curriculum – Resource Library

This document contains all recommended learning resources for the five-year curriculum.

Lessons throughout the curriculum may reference these resources.

The resources are organized by category rather than by year so they can be reused whenever appropriate.

---

# Books

## Beginner (Ages 10–12)

These books introduce money and business through stories and simple explanations.

### Personal Finance

* *Finance 101 for Kids* – Walter Andal
* *Money Ninja* – Mary Nhin
* *The Everything Kids' Money Book* – Brette Sember

### Entrepreneurship

* *Kid Start-Up* – Mark Cuban, Shaan Patel, Ian McCue

### Economics

* *Economics for Beginners* – Lara Bryan

---

## Intermediate (Ages 12–14)

* *The Psychology of Money* (selected chapters with discussion)
* *Rich Dad Poor Dad for Teens*
* *How to Turn $100 into $1,000,000*
* *The Motley Fool Investment Guide for Teens*

---

## Advanced (Ages 14–15)

* *The Intelligent Investor* (selected sections)
* *One Up on Wall Street*
* *The Little Book of Common Sense Investing*
* *Financial Intelligence*
* Selected company annual reports

# Biographies

Study great financial thinkers and entrepreneurs.

Suggested sequence:

* Warren Buffett
* Charlie Munger
* Benjamin Franklin
* John D. Rockefeller
* Henry Ford
* Steve Jobs
* Narayana Murthy
* Ratan Tata
* Dhirubhai Ambani
* Nikhil Kamath

Discuss:

* Successes
* Failures
* Habits
* Decisions
* Ethics

# Movies & Documentaries

Examples:

* The Boy Who Harnessed the Wind
* Inside Job (later years)

* The Billion Dollar Code

# Parent Library

Recommended reading for parents:

* The Psychology of Money
* The Millionaire Next Door
* The Richest Man in Babylon
* I Will Teach You to Be Rich
* The Simple Path to Wealth

Parents do not need to become finance experts, but reading alongside the child will enrich discussions.

---

# Resource Selection Principles

Every resource should be:

* Age-appropriate
* Factually accurate
* Practical
* Ethical
* Engaging
* Free or affordable where possible

Preference should be given to timeless resources that build enduring financial understanding rather than short-term market trends.
