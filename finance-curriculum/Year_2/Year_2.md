# Finance Mastery Curriculum

# Year 2 – Personal Finance

**Recommended Age:** 11–12 Years

**Duration:** 40 Learning Weeks

**Modules:** 10

---

# Theme

**Managing Money Wisely**

In Year 1, the student learned what money is and developed healthy financial habits.

Year 2 focuses on using money responsibly in everyday life.

Students will learn how banks work, how digital payments function, why governments collect taxes, how insurance protects people, why prices rise over time, and how planning today affects future financial security.

The emphasis is on making informed financial decisions and understanding the responsibilities that come with managing money.

---

# Year Objectives

By the end of Year 2, the student should be able to:

* Understand different banking services.
* Use digital payment methods safely.
* Explain why governments collect taxes.
* Understand the purpose of insurance.
* Explain inflation in simple terms.
* Calculate simple and compound interest.
* Distinguish between good debt and bad debt.
* Create short-term and long-term financial plans.
* Understand the importance of emergency savings.
* Make responsible financial decisions in daily life.

# Year-Long Practical Activities

Throughout the year, the student will maintain:

* Personal Budget Journal
* Savings Goal Tracker
* Inflation Observation Log
* Banking Vocabulary Notebook
* Financial News Scrapbook
* Compound Interest Practice Book

These activities encourage consistent application of financial concepts.

---

# Major Project

**My Personal Financial Plan**

The student will create a complete personal financial plan that includes:

* Income sources (real or simulated)
* Monthly budget
* Savings goals
* Emergency fund
* Spending analysis
* Financial priorities
* Reflection on financial habits

The project encourages thoughtful planning and responsible money management.

---

# Assessment Plan

* Weekly reviews
* Module-end quizzes (10)
* Practical banking and budgeting activities
* Parent discussions
* Mid-year practical assessment
* Year-end capstone project
* Final Year 2 assessment

Assessments focus on applying financial knowledge in realistic situations.

---

# Expected Outcomes

By the end of Year 2, the student should confidently:

* Manage a simple personal budget.
* Explain how banks and digital payments work.
* Understand taxes and insurance at a basic level.
* Calculate simple and compound interest.
* Explain inflation and its effects.
* Make informed financial choices.
* Plan for future financial goals.
* Demonstrate responsible financial habits.
* Be fully prepared to begin Year 3: Business & Investing.
