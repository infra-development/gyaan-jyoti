# Finance Mastery Curriculum

# Year 3 – Business & Investing

**Recommended Age:** 12–13 Years

**Duration:** 40 Learning Weeks

**Modules:** 10

---

# Theme

**Understanding How Businesses Create Wealth**

In Year 2, the student learned how to manage personal finances responsibly.

Year 3 shifts the focus from managing money to understanding how money is created through businesses, entrepreneurship, and investing.

Students will discover how companies earn revenue, generate profit, manage costs, and raise capital. They will also learn the fundamentals of investing, including stocks, mutual funds, bonds, ETFs, and financial statements.

The emphasis is on developing an investor's mindset—understanding businesses before investing in them.

---

# Year Objectives

By the end of Year 3, the student should be able to:

* Explain how businesses operate.
* Understand different business models.
* Differentiate between revenue, expenses, profit, and cash flow.
* Understand entrepreneurship and value creation.
* Read simple financial statements.
* Explain why companies issue shares.
* Understand the stock market and stock exchanges.
* Compare stocks, mutual funds, bonds, and ETFs.
* Build and manage a virtual investment portfolio.
* Make simple investment decisions based on reasoning rather than emotions.

# Year-Long Practical Activities

Throughout the year, the student will maintain:

* Business Observation Journal
* Investment Journal
* Virtual Stock Portfolio
* Company Analysis Notebook
* Financial Statement Practice Book
* Business Vocabulary Notebook

These activities help connect classroom learning with real-world businesses and investments.

---

# Major Project

**Build and Manage a Virtual Investment Portfolio**

The student will:

* Select companies for a virtual portfolio.
* Record reasons for each investment.
* Track performance throughout the year.
* Compare different investment options.
* Maintain an investment journal.
* Present lessons learned at the end of the year.

The emphasis is on developing sound investment reasoning rather than maximizing returns.

# Expected Outcomes

By the end of Year 3, the student should confidently:

* Explain how businesses create value.
* Understand entrepreneurship fundamentals.
* Read basic financial statements.
* Explain the purpose of stock markets.
* Compare different investment products.
* Build and justify a diversified virtual portfolio.
* Analyze simple businesses using financial information.
* Develop the mindset of a thoughtful long-term investor.
* Be fully prepared to begin Year 4: Advanced Finance.
