# Finance Mastery Curriculum

# Year 4 – Advanced Finance & Wealth Building

**Recommended Age:** 13–14 Years

**Duration:** 40 Learning Weeks

**Modules:** 10

---

# Theme

**Thinking Like an Investor and Financial Analyst**

By the beginning of Year 4, the student understands money, personal finance, businesses, and the fundamentals of investing.

Year 4 focuses on evaluating businesses, managing investments, understanding economies, and making informed financial decisions using data, logic, and evidence.

Students will explore corporate finance, company valuation, portfolio management, macroeconomics, behavioral finance, real estate, commodities, and international finance.

The emphasis shifts from learning financial concepts to applying them in realistic situations.

---

# Year Objectives

By the end of Year 4, the student should be able to:

* Explain how companies raise and manage capital.
* Understand why businesses have different valuations.
* Analyze businesses using financial information.
* Build and manage diversified investment portfolios.
* Explain major economic indicators and their impact.
* Understand the relationship between inflation, interest rates, and the economy.
* Compare different asset classes.
* Recognize common behavioral biases in financial decisions.
* Understand global financial markets.
* Make disciplined, evidence-based investment decisions.

* Economic News Journal
* Company Valuation Notebook
* Financial Ratio Workbook
* Decision Reflection Journal

These activities encourage disciplined analysis and long-term thinking.

---

# Major Project

**Complete Investment Research Report**

The student will:

* Select one publicly listed company.
* Study its business model.
* Analyze its financial statements.
* Calculate basic financial ratios.
* Evaluate strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and risks.
* Estimate a reasonable valuation using age-appropriate methods.
* Decide whether they would invest and justify their decision.
* Present the report to parents or mentors.

The emphasis is on logical reasoning, structured analysis, and evidence-based conclusions rather than predicting stock prices.
