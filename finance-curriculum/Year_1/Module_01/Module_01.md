# Year 1 – Module 1

# The Story of Money

**Duration:** 4 Weeks

**Recommended Age:** 10–11 Years

---

# Module Theme

**Understanding Why Humans Invented Money**

Money is one of humanity's greatest inventions.

Every day we use money to buy food, clothes, books, toys, and countless other things. Yet very few people stop to ask a simple question:

> **Why does money exist?**

This module takes students on a journey through history to discover how people lived before money, why trade became necessary, why barter eventually failed, and how money evolved into the system we use today.

Rather than memorizing definitions, students will experience the story of money through discussions, activities, simulations, and real-world observations.

By understanding the origin of money, students build a strong foundation for every topic that follows in the next five years.

---

# Why This Module Matters

Every future financial concept depends on understanding money.

Students cannot fully understand:

* Saving
* Spending
* Banks
* Investing
* Businesses
* Economics

unless they first understand **why money was invented**.

This module builds that foundation.

---

# Module Goals

By the end of this module, students should understand:

* Why people trade.
* Why barter was created.
* Why barter eventually failed.
* How money solved many trading problems.
* Why society trusts money.
* Why money continues to evolve.

---

# Learning Outcomes

After completing this module, the student will be able to:

* Explain the history of money in simple language.
* Describe the barter system and its limitations.
* Explain why money was invented.
* Identify different forms of money throughout history.
* Explain why people accept money in exchange for goods and services.
* Recognize that money is a tool—not the ultimate goal.

# Weekly Roadmap

## Week 1 — The Birth of Trade

### Big Question

**How did people live before money existed?**

Topics:

* Human needs
* Human wants
* Living in communities
* Division of work
* Goods
* Services
* Trade
* Problems without trade
* Introduction to barter

Outcome:

Students understand why trade naturally developed.

---

## Week 2 — The Barter Revolution

### Big Question

**Why wasn't barter enough?**

Topics:

* The barter system
* Double coincidence of wants
* Fair exchange
* Commodity money
* Salt
* Cattle
* Shells
* Gold
* Silver
* Why commodity money evolved

Outcome:

Students understand the strengths and weaknesses of barter and why societies searched for a better system.

---

## Week 3 — The Evolution of Money

### Big Question

**How did money change over time?**

Topics:

* Metal coins
* Paper money
* Banks
* Currency
* Digital money
* Debit cards
* UPI
* Mobile payments
* Money around the world
* The future of money

Outcome:

Students understand how money evolved from physical objects to digital systems.

---

## Week 4 — Why Money Works

### Big Question

**Why do people trust money?**

Topics:

* Trust
* Government
* Currency
* Counterfeit money
* Money supply (basic)
* Inflation (very simple introduction)
* Review
* Project
* Assessment

Outcome:

Students understand that money works because people trust it and society agrees to use it.
