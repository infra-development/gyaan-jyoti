# Lesson 1 – Imagine a World Without Money

**Estimated Duration:** 45 Minutes

---

# Big Question

> **What would happen if money suddenly disappeared tomorrow?**

Today is not about finding the answer.

Today is about asking the right question.

---

# Lesson Theme

Money is so common that we rarely stop to think about it.

Every day we use it.

Every day we see it.

But has it always existed?

Imagine waking up tomorrow morning and discovering that nobody can use money anymore.

What would happen?

Today, we begin exploring that idea.

---

# Learning Objectives

By the end of today's lesson, the student will be able to:

* Recognize that money is a human invention.
* Imagine life without money.
* Identify basic human needs.
* Ask questions about how people survived before money existed.

---

# Warm-Up Activity (5 Minutes)

Close your eyes.

Imagine this announcement appears on television:

> **"Starting today, money no longer exists anywhere in the world."**

Take one minute to think.

Now answer:

* How would you buy breakfast?
* How would you get to school?
* Could your parents buy vegetables?
* Could anyone buy petrol?
* What would happen at the supermarket?

There are no wrong answers.

# Story Time

## A Strange Morning

Aryan woke up early.

His mother handed him his school bag.

His father picked up his wallet.

When they reached the grocery shop, everyone looked confused.

The shopkeeper smiled sadly.

> "I'm sorry."

> "Money doesn't exist anymore."

People looked at each other.

One person offered shoes.

Another offered vegetables.

Someone wanted milk.

Someone had rice.

Nobody knew how to exchange things fairly.

By afternoon, almost every shop had closed.

The entire town was confused.

Aryan wondered...

> **"If money disappeared today, how would people survive?"**

That is the question we will explore over the next few weeks.

# Think Like a Detective

Discuss these questions.

There are no perfect answers.

1. Why do people need food every day?
2. Could one person grow food, build a house, make clothes, teach children and become a doctor all at once?
3. Why do people help each other?
4. Why do we depend on other people every day?

# New Concepts

Today we learn only two important ideas.

## Need

A **need** is something we must have to live safely and healthily.

Examples:

* Food
* Water
* Clothing
* Shelter
* Medicine

Without needs, life becomes difficult.

---

## Want

A **want** is something we would like to have but can live without.

Examples:

* Ice cream
* Toys
* Video games
* Bicycle upgrades
* Chocolates

Wants make life enjoyable, but they are not necessary for survival.

# Lesson Summary

Today we learned:

* People have needs and wants.
* We use money every day.
* Money did not always exist.
* Life without money would be very different.
* We depend on many other people every day.

Next lesson, we will discover **why humans began working together instead of trying to do everything by themselves.**

# Parent Notes (For Adults)

### Today's Goal

Do **not** explain the history of money.

Allow the child to think, imagine, and ask questions.

If they ask, "What is the correct answer?", respond with:

> "Let's discover it together over the next few lessons."

Curiosity is more valuable than memorizing facts in this first lesson.
