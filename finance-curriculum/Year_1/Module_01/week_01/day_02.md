# Big Question

> **Why do people depend on each other instead of doing everything themselves?**

# Story Time

## The Boy Who Wanted to Do Everything

A boy named Arjun believed he didn't need anyone.

"I can do everything myself," he proudly said.

On Monday, he tried growing wheat.

On Tuesday, he tried making shoes.

On Wednesday, he built a chair.

On Thursday, he tried catching fish.

On Friday, he wanted to become a doctor because he caught a cold.

By Saturday, he was exhausted.

His grandfather smiled and said,

> "The strongest people are not those who do everything themselves. They are those who work well with others."

Arjun finally understood that every person has different skills, and together a community can achieve much more than any individual alone.

# New Concepts

## Skills

A skill is something a person learns to do well.

Examples:

* Cooking
* Teaching
* Farming
* Building houses
* Repairing machines

Everyone has different skills.

---

## Cooperation

Cooperation means working together to achieve a common goal.

Examples:

* Parents preparing dinner together.
* Doctors and nurses caring for patients.
* Engineers and construction workers building a bridge.

Communities become stronger when people cooperate.

---

## Professions

A profession is the work or occupation a person does.

Examples:

* Farmer
* Teacher
* Doctor
* Carpenter
* Tailor
* Electrician

Each profession helps society in a different way.

---

# Parent Notes (For Adults)

The goal of today's lesson is not to teach jobs.

The deeper lesson is that **specialization increases productivity and cooperation makes society stronger**.

Do not use those technical words yet.

Instead, help your child discover this idea naturally through examples from everyday life.
