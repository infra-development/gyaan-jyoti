# Year 1 → Module 1 → Week 1

# Week 1 – The Birth of Trade

**Duration:** 4 Hours

**Lessons:** 5

---

# Theme

**The Beginning of Human Exchange**

Long before coins, paper money, banks, or mobile payments existed, humans still had needs. They learned to cooperate, specialize, and exchange goods and services. This week explores how trade naturally emerged and why it became essential for civilization.

---

# Big Question

> **How did people meet their needs before money existed?**

Everything learned this week should help answer this question.

---

# Weekly Goal

By the end of the week, the student should understand:

* Why humans have needs and wants.
* Why people cannot produce everything themselves.
* Why communities formed.
* How specialization led to trade.
* Why trade became an essential part of everyday life.

---

# Learning Objectives

After completing this week, the student will be able to:

* Explain the difference between needs and wants (basic introduction).
* Describe why people began working together.
* Explain specialization in simple language.
* Distinguish between goods and services.
* Explain why people started trading.

# Daily Roadmap

## Day 1 — Imagine a World Without Money

### Big Idea

Money did not always exist.

### Concepts

* Human life long ago
* Basic human needs
* Living without shops
* Living without money

### Activity

Imagine living for one day without money.

---

## Day 2 — Humans Cannot Do Everything

### Big Idea

People survive better by helping each other.

### Concepts

* Different skills
* Farmers
* Hunters
* Builders
* Cooperation

### Activity

Identify different jobs performed by people in your family and neighborhood.

---

## Day 3 — Goods, Services & Specialization

### Big Idea

Different people create different value.

### Concepts

* Goods
* Services
* Producers
* Consumers
* Division of work

### Activity

Sort 20 everyday examples into "Goods" or "Services."

---

## Day 4 — The Birth of Trade

### Big Idea

People began exchanging goods and services because no one could produce everything.

### Concepts

* Exchange
* Trade
* Fair exchange
* Everyday examples

### Activity

Family trading game using household items or printed cards.

---

## Day 5 — Weekly Review & Mini Project

### Review

Review all concepts learned during the week.

### Quiz

Vocabulary

Goods vs. Services

Trade scenarios

Short answers

### Mini Project

Draw your own village.

Include:

* Farmers
* Carpenter
* Potter
* Tailor
* Teacher
* Doctor

Show how they help each other by exchanging goods and services.

Present your drawing to your family.

Students practice:

Students practice:

* Listening to a story.

# Critical Thinking

Discuss:

* Can one person build an entire city alone?
* Why do people have different jobs?
* What would happen if nobody grew food?
* Why is helping each other important?

Students should explain **why**, not just answer.

# Parent Activity

Walk through your home together.

Choose ten objects.

For each object ask:

* Who made it?
* What skills were required?
* Could our family make it ourselves?
* If not, how did we get it?

Discuss how many different people contribute to everyday life.
