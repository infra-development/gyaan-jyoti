# Finance Mastery Curriculum

# Year 1 – Foundations of Money

**Recommended Age:** 10–11 Years

**Duration:** 40 Learning Weeks

**Modules:** 10

---

# Theme

**Understanding Money and Building Healthy Financial Habits**

Year 1 introduces the student to the world of money.

The emphasis is not on investing or becoming wealthy, but on understanding why money exists, how it is used responsibly, and how good financial habits are formed.

This year lays the foundation for every topic in the remaining four years.

---

# Year Objectives

By the end of Year 1, the student should be able to:

* Explain what money is.
* Explain why money was invented.
* Distinguish between needs and wants.
* Make thoughtful spending decisions.
* Develop a regular saving habit.
* Understand simple budgeting.
* Explain the basic purpose of banks.
* Understand the idea of earning money by creating value.
* Recognize that time influences money.
* Demonstrate honesty and responsibility when handling money.

# Year-Long Practical Activities

Throughout the year, the student will maintain:

* A Money Journal
* A Savings Tracker
* A Spending Tracker
* A Vocabulary Notebook
* A Finance Scrapbook (optional)

These activities encourage continuous reflection and practical application.

---

# Major Project

**My First Financial Journal**

The student will build a personal journal documenting:

* Saving goals
* Spending decisions
* Family discussions
* Activities
* Reflections
* Lessons learned
* Mini-projects

The journal will serve as a record of the student's financial growth throughout the year.

---

# Assessment Plan

* Weekly reviews
* Module-end quizzes (10)
* Practical activities
* Parent discussions
* Year-end capstone project
* Final Year 1 assessment

Assessment emphasizes understanding, application, and good habits rather than memorization.

---

# Expected Outcomes

By the end of Year 1, the student should confidently:

* Use financial vocabulary correctly.
* Explain the purpose of money.
* Save consistently toward a goal.
* Make simple budgets.
* Spend thoughtfully.
* Understand the basic role of banks.
* Demonstrate responsible financial behavior.
* Be fully prepared to begin Year 2: Personal Finance.
