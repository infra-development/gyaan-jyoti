# Week 1 Final Business Challenge
## Your First Week as a Data Analyst at Pagila DVD Rentals

---

# Welcome

Congratulations!

Today is your first day as a **Junior Data Analyst** at **Pagila DVD Rentals**.

After completing your onboarding, your manager says:

> "Welcome to the team! Before tomorrow's executive meeting, every department has requested a few reports. These reports cover everything you've learned in Week 1.
>
> Don't rush into SQL.
>
> First understand the business problem.
>
> Then identify the SQL concepts.
>
> Finally write the query."

This challenge is designed to simulate a real working day.

---

# Rules

For every request:

## Step 1

Identify the Business Objective.

Example:

- Retrieve information
- Measure performance
- Compare groups
- Find missing information

---

## Step 2

Identify the Business Entities.

Examples:

- Customer
- Film
- Inventory
- Payment
- Rental
- Store
- Category
- Actor

---

## Step 3

Identify the Thinking Pattern.

Possible answers:

- Retrieval
- Filtering
- Sorting
- Summarization
- Grouping
- Basic Join

One request may require multiple patterns.

---

## Step 4

Identify the SQL concepts required.

Examples:

- SELECT
- WHERE
- ORDER BY
- Aggregate Functions
- GROUP BY
- HAVING
- INNER JOIN
- LEFT JOIN

---

## Step 5

Write the SQL.

# Completion Criteria

You are ready for Week 2 if you can confidently:

- Retrieve data from a table.
- Filter rows correctly.
- Sort results.
- Calculate business metrics.
- Group data correctly.
- Use HAVING appropriately.
- Write INNER JOIN queries.
- Write simple LEFT JOIN queries.
- Navigate the Pagila schema confidently.
- Translate business questions into SQL without guessing.

---

# Final Advice

Don't measure your progress by the number of SQL queries you can write.

Measure it by how quickly you can answer these two questions:

> **What is the business trying to achieve?**

and

> **Which SQL thinking pattern solves this problem?**

When those answers become obvious, writing SQL becomes much easier.
